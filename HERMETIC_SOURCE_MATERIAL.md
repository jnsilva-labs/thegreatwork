# Hermetic, Alchemical & Esoteric Source Material
## Raw Reference for Website Copy

This document collects verified source language, phrases, and concepts from primary and historical texts in the Hermetic, alchemical, astrological, and esoteric traditions. Each entry includes the source attribution and a note on how it might be adapted into warm, accessible website copy.

---

## 1. THE KYBALION (1908)

**Source:** *The Kybalion: A Study of the Hermetic Philosophy of Ancient Egypt and Greece*, attributed to "Three Initiates" (likely William Walker Atkinson), published 1908.

### The Seven Hermetic Principles

**Opening Axiom:**
> "The Principles of Truth are Seven; he who knows these, understandingly, possesses the Magic Key before whose touch all the Doors of the Temple fly open."

**Copy note:** The metaphor of a "magic key" and "doors of the temple" can frame the whole site experience -- the idea that these tools (tarot, astrology, sacred geometry) are keys that open doors to self-understanding.

---

**I. The Principle of Mentalism:**
> "THE ALL is MIND; The Universe is Mental."

**Copy note:** This grounds the idea that consciousness shapes reality. Could frame meditation/mindfulness features or intention-setting tools. "Your mind is not separate from the cosmos -- it participates in it."

---

**II. The Principle of Correspondence:**
> "As above, so below; as below, so above."

**Copy note:** The most famous Hermetic axiom. Perfect for connecting personal inner work to cosmic patterns. "The patterns in the stars mirror the patterns within you." Bridges astrology to self-knowledge naturally.

---

**III. The Principle of Vibration:**
> "Nothing rests; everything moves; everything vibrates."

**Copy note:** Useful for framing energy work, sound, or the dynamic nature of tarot readings. "Everything is in motion -- including you. A reading captures a moment in that movement."

---

**IV. The Principle of Polarity:**
> "Everything is Dual; everything has poles; everything has its pair of opposites; like and unlike are the same; opposites are identical in nature, but different in degree; extremes meet; all truths are but half-truths; all paradoxes may be reconciled."

**Copy note:** Rich language for discussing shadow work, tarot reversals, or the relationship between light and dark in alchemical transformation. "Opposites are not enemies -- they are the same force expressing itself differently."

---

**V. The Principle of Rhythm:**
> "Everything flows, out and in; everything has its tides; all things rise and fall; the pendulum-swing manifests in everything; the measure of the swing to the right is the measure of the swing to the left; rhythm compensates."

**Copy note:** Beautiful language for normalizing cycles -- emotional, seasonal, astrological. "Life has tides. What recedes will return. What rises will settle. This is not failure -- it is rhythm."

---

**VI. The Principle of Cause and Effect:**
> "Every Cause has its Effect; every Effect has its Cause; everything happens according to Law; Chance is but a name for Law not recognized; there are many planes of causation, but nothing escapes the Law."

**Copy note:** Frames the idea that nothing in a reading is "random." "There is no chance in the cards you draw -- only patterns not yet recognized."

---

**VII. The Principle of Gender:**
> "Gender is in everything; everything has its masculine and feminine principles; gender manifests on all planes."

**Copy note:** Useful for discussing the union of opposites in alchemy (Red King/White Queen), or the receptive/active qualities in all beings. Best handled with nuance -- frame as creative/receptive rather than gendered identity.

## 2. THE EMERALD TABLET OF HERMES TRISMEGISTUS

### Isaac Newton's Translation (c. 1680)

**Source:** Newton's translation from the Latin, found in his alchemical papers. The Emerald Tablet (Tabula Smaragdina) first appeared in Arabic sources from the late 8th or early 9th century. The Latin version (from which Newton translated) dates to the 12th century.

> "Tis true without lying, certain and most true. That which is below is like that which is above and that which is above is like that which is below to do the miracles of one only thing."

**Copy note:** "True without lying, certain and most true" -- this opening carries tremendous authority. It could introduce a section on tradition and lineage. The phrase "the miracles of one only thing" speaks to unity beneath apparent diversity.

---

> "And as all things have been and arose from one by the mediation of one: so all things have their birth from this one thing by adaptation."

**Copy note:** The idea of adaptation from a single source is a beautiful way to frame why one tradition (Hermeticism) branches into astrology, tarot, alchemy, sacred geometry -- "all things have their birth from this one thing by adaptation."

---

> "The Sun is its father, the moon its mother, the wind hath carried it in its belly, the earth is its nurse."

**Copy note:** Vivid elemental imagery. Could frame the four elements or the astrological modalities. The cosmos as a family -- Sun as father, Moon as mother -- speaks to the personal relationship with celestial bodies.

---

> "It ascends from the earth to the heaven and again it descends to the earth and receives the force of things superior and inferior."

**Copy note:** The ascending/descending motion mirrors the soul's journey in Hermetic cosmology. "Wisdom rises from the body to the spirit and returns again, enriched." Perfect for describing the purpose of contemplative practice.

---

> "Separate thou the earth from the fire, the subtle from the gross sweetly with great industry."

**Copy note:** "Sweetly with great industry" -- transformation requires effort but should be done gently. This is the tone the whole site should strike: earnest but not harsh.

---

### The Fuller "As Above, So Below" Context

**Latin original:** "Quod est superius est sicut quod inferius, et quod inferius est sicut quod est superius."

**Literal translation:** "That which is above is like to that which is below, and that which is below is like to that which is above."

**Arabic original nuance:** The Arabic reads "from" rather than "like to" -- "That which is above is FROM that which is below, and that which is below is FROM that which is above." This suggests not mere similarity but actual origin and interconnection.

**Copy note:** The Arabic version is actually richer for website copy -- it implies that you are not merely *like* the cosmos but *from* the cosmos. "You are not a reflection of the stars -- you are made from the same source."

## 3. THE CORPUS HERMETICUM

**Source:** The Corpus Hermeticum is a collection of 17 Greek treatises written between approximately 100-300 CE. The standard English translation is by G.R.S. Mead (1906), in the public domain. The Asclepius (or Perfect Discourse) survives primarily in Latin translation.

### From the Poimandres (Treatise I: "The Shepherd of Men")

**On the Nature of God:**
> "That Light, He said, am I, thy God, Mind, prior to Moist Nature which appeared from Darkness; the Light-Word (Logos) that appeared from Mind is Son of God."

**Copy note:** Light, Mind, and Word (Logos) as aspects of the divine. "Before anything was formed, there was Light and Mind. You carry both within you."

---

**On the Nature of God as Life and Light:**
> "God-the-Mind, being male and female both, as Light and Life subsisting, brought forth another Mind to give things form."

**Copy note:** The divine as both Light AND Life, both creative AND sustaining. This frames the active/receptive duality without rigid gender categories.

---

**On Self-Knowledge and Deathlessness:**
> "The man who hath Mind in him, let him learn to know that he himself is deathless."

**Copy note:** Powerful and direct. Self-knowledge reveals something eternal in you. "To know yourself is to touch what cannot die."

---

**On the Presence of Mind:**
> "I, Mind, myself am present with holy men and good, the pure and merciful, men who live piously. To such my presence doth become an aid, and straightway they gain gnosis of all things, and win the Father's love by their pure lives."

**Copy note:** Mind (Nous) is not something you acquire -- it is something that draws near when you live well. "Wisdom does not need to be forced. It arrives when you make space for it."

---

**On Returning to Light:**
> "If then thou learnest that thou art thyself of Life and Light, and that thou happen'st to be out of them, thou shalt return again to Life."

**Copy note:** You are already made of what you seek. The journey is not acquisition but return. "You are already made of Light and Life. The work is simply remembering."

---

### From The Cup or Monad (Treatise IV)

**The Cup of Mind Sent Down:**
> "Baptize thyself with this Cup's baptism, what heart can do so, thou that hast faith thou canst ascend to him that hath sent down the Cup, thou that dost know for what thou didst come into being!"

**Copy note:** The divine offers knowledge, but you must choose to receive it. "The wisdom is already here. The question is whether you are willing to receive it."

---

**Those Who Received Mind:**
> "As many then as understood the Herald's tidings and doused themselves in Mind, became partakers in the Gnosis."

**Copy note:** "Doused themselves in Mind" -- an extraordinary image. Not intellectual learning but immersion. "Understanding is not reading about the water. It is stepping into it."

---

### From The Key (Treatise X)

**On the Virtue of the Soul:**
> "The virtue of the soul is Gnosis. For he who knows, he good and pious is, and still while on the earth divine."

**Copy note:** Knowledge IS virtue. Knowing yourself makes you good. "Self-knowledge is not a luxury -- it is how you become who you are meant to be."

---

**On Human Divinity:**
> "Man on earth is god subject to death, while god in heaven is man from death immune."

**Copy note:** A stunning paradox. Humans are mortal gods; God is an immortal human. "You are not trying to become divine. You already are -- just temporarily clothed in mortality."

---

**On Ignorance:**
> "The soul's vice is ignorance... bearing the body as a load; not as the ruler, but the ruled."

**Copy note:** Without self-knowledge, the soul is carried by circumstance rather than directing it. "Without understanding yourself, life carries you. With it, you carry yourself."

---

### From Mind Unto Hermes (Treatise XI)

**On Expanding Consciousness:**
> "Think that thou art at the same time in every place -- in earth, in sea, in sky; not yet begotten, in the womb, young, old, and dead."

**Copy note:** An extraordinary meditation instruction from 2,000 years ago. "Imagine yourself everywhere at once -- in every age, every place, every state of being. That is how the Mind sees."

---

**On Transcendence:**
> "Become more lofty than all height, and lower than all depth. Collect into thyself all senses of all creatures."

**Copy note:** Not detachment FROM life but radical expansion INTO all experience. "Do not withdraw from the world. Expand until you contain it."

---

### From the Asclepius (The Perfect Discourse)

**The Great Miracle of Humanity:**
> "Man is a mighty wonder -- an animal meet for our worship and for our respect."

Alternative translation: "What a great miracle is Man, O Asclepius."

Latin: "Magnum miraculum est homo."

**Copy note:** This phrase was quoted by Pico della Mirandola to open his *Oration on the Dignity of Man* (1486), one of the founding documents of Renaissance humanism. The idea that a human being is itself a miracle -- not because of what it achieves but because of what it IS -- is the philosophical foundation of the entire site.

## 4. ALCHEMICAL STAGES (THE MAGNUM OPUS)

**Source:** The four-stage model (Nigredo, Albedo, Citrinitas, Rubedo) can be traced to at least the 1st century CE. Zosimos of Panopolis attributed knowledge of this sequence to Mary the Jewess. The development of black, white, yellow, and red also appears in the *Physika kai Mystika* of Pseudo-Democritus, one of the oldest surviving alchemical texts.

### NIGREDO (Blackening) -- The First Stage

**The Rosarium Philosophorum (16th century):**
> "When you see your matter going black, rejoice: you are at the beginning of the work."

**Thomas Browne, *Hydriotaphia* (1658):**
The nigredo stage described as "lost in the uncomfortable night of nothing."

**Alchemical operations:** mortificatio (killing), putrefactio (rotting), calcinatio (burning), iteratio (repetition). The *modus operandi* is described as "slow, repetitive, difficult, desiccating, severe."

**Key symbols:** The black sun (sol niger), the raven, the skull, decomposition, the prima materia.

**Copy note:** The nigredo maps perfectly to the "dark night of the soul" -- those periods of confusion, dissolution, or crisis that precede transformation. "When everything falls apart, the alchemists say: rejoice. This is the beginning." The Rosarium Philosophorum quote is gold for copy.

---

### ALBEDO (Whitening) -- The Second Stage

**Alchemical term:** ablutio -- literally, "the washing away of impurities."

**Process:** Following the chaos (massa confusa) of the nigredo, the alchemist undertakes purification. The matter turns "a brilliant white."

**Key symbols:** The white swan, the white rose, the white queen, Luna (the Moon), philosophical silver.

**Description:** "When the matter reaches the albedo, it has become pure and spotless."

**Copy note:** The albedo is the emergence of clarity after confusion. "After the darkness, a washing. After the washing, something clean and new begins to appear." The moon symbolism connects naturally to lunar cycles and receptive awareness.

---

### CITRINITAS (Yellowing) -- The Third Stage

**Symbolism:** The dawning of "solar light" inherent in one's being. The reflective "lunar or soul light" gives way to direct solar illumination.

**Key symbol:** The cauda pavonis (peacock's tail) -- a burst of iridescent colors signaling multiplicity before unity.

**Paracelsus:** Connected citrinitas to the element of air and the planet Jupiter -- expansion and knowledge.

**Historical note:** After the 15th century, many alchemical authors compressed citrinitas into rubedo, treating the four stages as three. This is itself meaningful -- awakening is so close to completion that it can be hard to distinguish from it.

**Copy note:** "There is a moment between the clearing and the completion when everything becomes luminous. The alchemists called it the yellowing -- the first light of dawn within you." The peacock's tail image is spectacular for visual design.

---

### RUBEDO (Reddening) -- The Fourth and Final Stage

**Description:** Both gold and the Philosopher's Stone were associated with the color red. Rubedo signaled alchemical success and the end of the Great Work.

**The Coniunctio (Sacred Marriage):** The final stage unites opposites: white queen and red king, mercury and sulfur, masculine and feminine, spirit and matter. This union produces the Philosopher's Stone -- the unified self that transcends duality.

**Key symbols:** The Red King, the Philosopher's Stone, gold, the phoenix, the chemical wedding (chymische Hochzeit).

**Copy note:** "The Great Work does not end in perfection -- it ends in wholeness. Not the removal of darkness, but the marriage of dark and light." The coniunctio is the deep theme underneath every tarot reading that asks about integration.

---

### SOLVE ET COAGULA

**Meaning:** "Dissolve and coagulate" -- the essential alchemical process.

**Solve (solutio):** Breaking down elements, dissolving fixed structures.
**Coagula:** Bringing together, binding, fixing into new form.

**Paracelsus on transmutation:**
> "If the Tincture of the Philosophers is to be used for transmutation, a pound of it must be projected on a thousand pounds of melted Sol. Then, at length, will a medicine have been prepared for transmuting the leprous moisture of the metals."

**Copy note:** "Every transformation has two movements: the dissolving of what was, and the forming of what will be. You cannot have one without the other." Solve et coagula is the rhythm of all change -- teardown and rebuild.

## 5. TAROT HISTORY & ESOTERIC ORIGINS

### Antoine Court de Gebelin (1781)

**Source:** *Le Monde primitif, analyse et compare avec le monde moderne* (The Primeval World, Analyzed and Compared with the Modern World), Volume VIII, 1781.

**Core claim:** Court de Gebelin proposed (without historical evidence) that tarot cards were a surviving fragment of the ancient Egyptian *Book of Thoth*, that Egyptian priests had distilled into symbolic images. He claimed these were brought to Rome, secretly known to the popes, carried to Avignon in the 14th century, and introduced to France from there.

**Historical assessment:** Later Egyptologists found nothing in Egyptian language or culture to support these claims. Despite being factually incorrect, his writing exerted a profound influence on all subsequent esoteric tarot interpretation.

**Copy note:** The value here is not historical accuracy but the *impulse* -- the sense that the cards contain something older and deeper than a game. "In 1781, a French scholar looked at a deck of playing cards and saw the remnants of ancient wisdom. He was wrong about the history -- but perhaps right about the depth."

---

### Eliphas Levi (1854-1856)

**Source:** *Dogme et Rituel de la Haute Magie* (Transcendental Magic: Its Doctrine and Ritual), 1854-1856.

**On the Tarot as a philosophical instrument:**
> "A truly philosophical machine, which keeps the mind from wandering, while leaving its initiative and liberty; it is mathematics applied to the Absolute, the alliance of the positive and the ideal, a lottery of thoughts as exact as numbers."

**Copy note:** "A lottery of thoughts as exact as numbers" -- this single phrase captures what a good tarot reading feels like: seemingly random yet precisely meaningful.

---

**On the Tarot as universal key:**
> "An imprisoned person, with no other book than the Tarot, if he knew how to use it, could in a few years acquire universal knowledge, and would be able to speak on all subjects with unequalled learning and inexhaustible eloquence."

**Copy note:** This is hyperbolic but captures the Hermetic idea that the tarot contains ALL the patterns. "The 78 cards contain a complete map of human experience."

---

**On the Tarot's structure:**
Levi described the tarot as "a hieroglyphic and numeral alphabet, expressing by characters and numbers a series of universal and absolute ideas."

He structured *Dogme et Rituel* in twenty-two chapters, each marked by a Hebrew letter, establishing the correspondence between the 22 Major Arcana and the 22 letters of the Hebrew alphabet.

**Copy note:** Levi's key contribution was connecting tarot to Kabbalah. This is where the system of correspondences begins -- each card as a letter in a cosmic alphabet.

---

### The Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn (1888-c.1903)

**Source:** *Book T: A Description of the Cards of the Tarot with their Attributions; Including a Method of Divination by Their Use*, written primarily by S.L. MacGregor Mathers, c. 1891. Part of the Second Order curriculum of the Ordo Rosae Rubeae et Aureae Crucis ("The Order of the Ruby Rose and Golden Cross").

**Significance:** Book T established a comprehensive system of correspondences between tarot trumps, Hebrew letters, pathways on the Kabbalistic Tree of Life, astrological signs, planets, and elements. This is the framework that underlies virtually all modern esoteric tarot practice.

**Practice:** Golden Dawn members were required to draw and color their own tarot decks using Mathers' specifications from Book T -- making the creation of the deck itself a ritual act of learning.

**Publication:** Aleister Crowley published the card designs publicly in *The Equinox* (1912). Israel Regardie released the full Book T papers in his compilation *The Golden Dawn* (late 1930s).

**Copy note:** "The Golden Dawn did not just read tarot cards -- they wove the cards into a vast web of correspondence connecting astrology, Kabbalah, alchemy, and sacred geometry. Every card became a node in a living map of reality." This is the historical justification for the site connecting these systems.

## 6. ASTROLOGICAL HERMETIC CONNECTIONS

### Cosmic Sympathy (Sympatheia)

**Source:** The concept of cosmic sympathy (Greek: sympatheia) originates in Stoic philosophy and was adopted by the Hermetic tradition. It holds that everything in the cosmos is connected, and that seemingly unrelated things can influence each other through invisible bonds of resonance.

**Core idea:** Because the heavens correspond to the individual, the configuration of the planets at a person's birth provides insight into their talents, health, challenges, and fate.

**Copy note:** "The ancients believed in cosmic sympathy -- that everything is connected to everything else through invisible threads of resonance. Your birth chart is where those threads become visible."

---

### Microcosm and Macrocosm

**Source:** The concept was articulated by Pythagoras (6th century BCE) and developed by Plato (4th century BCE). In Hermetic thought, the human being is a miniature cosmos (microcosm), and the universe is a great human (macrocosm).

**Connection to the Emerald Tablet:** "As above, so below" is the axiom of this relationship.

**Hermetic articulation:** The soul of the individual is linked to the World Soul (anima mundi). Alchemy applies the same principles to both macrocosm (transformation of materials) and microcosm (internal psychological and spiritual transformation).

**Copy note:** "You are not a speck in the universe. You are the universe in miniature. Every pattern out there has a corresponding pattern in here." This is the philosophical backbone of the entire site.

---

### The Soul's Journey Through the Planets

**Source:** Corpus Hermeticum I (Poimandres) and Hermetic astrological tradition.

**Concept:** In Hermetic cosmology, the soul descends from the divine through seven planetary spheres to incarnate on earth. At each sphere, the soul acquires qualities associated with that planet. After death, the purified soul ascends back through these spheres, surrendering the acquired passions and distortions at each level, before returning to the divine source.

**Planetary descending order (traditional):** Saturn, Jupiter, Mars, Sun, Venus, Mercury, Moon.

**Copy note:** "The planets are not just lights in the sky -- they are stages of the soul's journey. Each one gave you something when you arrived. Understanding your chart is understanding what you brought with you."

---

### Ptolemy's Tetrabiblos (2nd century CE)

**Source:** Claudius Ptolemy, *Tetrabiblos* (also called *Apotelesmatics* or *Quadripartitum*), c. 150 CE. The foundational text of Western astrological practice.

**Key contribution:** Ptolemy grounded astrology in natural philosophy -- the idea that celestial influence operates through physical law, not supernatural decree. To Ptolemy, astrology is a legitimate study because it operates according to observable natural patterns.

**Copy note:** "Astrology was not always 'mystical.' Ptolemy treated it as natural science -- the study of how celestial patterns correspond to earthly events through physical sympathy."

---

### Firmicus Maternus (4th century CE)

**Source:** Julius Firmicus Maternus, *Matheseos Libri VIII* (Eight Books of Astrology), c. 334 CE. One of the most important works of natal astrology from the Hellenistic era.

**Hermetic connection:** Firmicus claimed that the Thema Mundi (the birth chart of the world) was invented by Hermetic astrologers as an instructional tool, often employed as allegory.

**Copy note:** "The ancients cast a birth chart for the world itself -- mapping the cosmic arrangement at the moment of creation. If the universe has a chart, so do you."

## 7. SACRED GEOMETRY

### Plato's Timaeus (c. 360 BCE)

**Source:** Plato, *Timaeus*, the dialogue on cosmology and natural philosophy.

**The Demiurge and Creation:**
> "Good was he, and in one who is good there never arises about anything whatsoever any envy; and so, being free of this, he willed that all things should come to resemble himself as much as possible."

**Copy note:** Creation arises from generosity, not power. "The universe was not made from scarcity. It was made from an overflowing desire to share goodness." This frames the site's generosity of knowledge.

---

**The Cosmos as Living Being:**
The demiurge used as his model the "living being" (zÅion) that embraces all living beings within itself -- "just as this cosmos holds and embraces us."

**Copy note:** "The cosmos is not a machine. It is alive. It holds us the way a body holds its organs -- not as parts in a box but as members of a living whole."

---

**The Platonic Solids and Elements:**

Plato assigned a regular geometric solid to each classical element:
- **Fire** = Tetrahedron (4 triangular faces)
- **Air** = Octahedron (8 triangular faces)
- **Water** = Icosahedron (20 triangular faces)
- **Earth** = Cube (6 square faces)
- **The Cosmos/Aether** = Dodecahedron (12 pentagonal faces) -- "used for the universe as a whole," since it most nearly approaches the shape of a sphere.

**Key geometric insight:** Fire, air, and water can transform into each other because their faces are all composed of equilateral triangles (which can be broken down into smaller right triangles and recombined). Earth's cube faces are composed of different triangles (isosceles), so earth cannot transform into the other three.

**On beauty and geometry:**
> "He considered that the like is infinitely fairer than the unlike."

**Copy note:** "Plato saw the universe as geometry made flesh. Fire is a tetrahedron. Water is an icosahedron. The elements are not just substances -- they are shapes. The cosmos is, at its deepest level, a geometric thought."

---

### The Golden Ratio (Phi, approximately 1.618...)

**Euclid, *Elements* (c. 300 BCE):**
Euclid described the division of a line "in extreme and mean ratio" -- a division where the ratio of the whole to the larger part equals the ratio of the larger part to the smaller. This is the proportion now known as the golden ratio (Greek: akros kai mesos logos).

**Luca Pacioli, *De Divina Proportione* (1509):**
Written by Pacioli and illustrated by Leonardo da Vinci. Pacioli gave five reasons why the golden ratio should be called the "Divine Proportion":
1. Its value represents divine simplicity
2. Its definition invokes three lengths, symbolizing the Holy Trinity
3. Its irrationality represents God's incomprehensibility
4. Its self-similarity recalls God's omnipresence and invariability
5. (The fifth related to the dodecahedron and the quintessence)

**Copy note:** "The golden ratio is a number that cannot be fully expressed -- like the divine, it extends forever beyond our ability to capture it. And yet it appears everywhere: in shells, in flowers, in the proportions of your own body. A pattern that is infinite and intimate at once."

---

### The Flower of Life

**Location:** Five patterns of 19 overlapping circles in red ochre, found on granite columns at the Osirion (the cenotaph of Osiris) at Abydos, Egypt.

**Dating controversy:** The Osirion structure dates to the reign of Seti I (1294-1279 BCE). However, the Flower of Life symbols themselves were likely added during the Ptolemaic period (1st century BCE - 1st century CE), based on nearby Greek text. The symbols are painted in red ochre and are considered graffiti -- not part of the original temple design.

**Broader presence:** Versions of the pattern appear in Chinese, Indian, and Greek manuscripts, temples, and monuments across cultures.

**Geometric significance:** The Flower of Life is understood to contain within it the fundamental patterns that express the laws of nature, including the Platonic solids and the golden ratio.

**Copy note:** Honesty about the dating actually strengthens the narrative: "The Flower of Life appears across cultures and centuries -- painted by unknown hands on the walls of Egyptian temples, drawn in the margins of medieval manuscripts, carved into the floors of Renaissance churches. Its universality is not a mystery. It is geometry discovering itself."

---

### "God Geometrizes"

**Attribution:** This phrase is commonly attributed to Plato but does not appear verbatim in his surviving works. The closest source is Plutarch (c. 100 CE), who in *Quaestiones Convivales* (Table Talk) reports a discussion about why Plato said "God is always doing geometry" (aei geometrei ho theos).

**Copy note:** Use with attribution to the Platonic tradition rather than to Plato directly: "The Platonic tradition held that God is always doing geometry -- that the deepest structure of reality is mathematical, and that to study proportion and pattern is to study the mind of the divine."
